My Background



I worked several years in human resources and operations management, before deciding to pursue my passion of development. I taught myself for several months and joined General Assembly's Web Development bootcamp. Throughout the program, I built several MEAN stack and Ruby on Rails web apps. While I have enjoyed building all of them - the larger ones are in my portfolio - the one I am most proud of is probably my first; where during just my fifth week of the program, I built a basic platforming game. I challenged myself to both use the tools I had learned in the classroom and teach myself several entirely new frameworks and libraries; particularly the Quintus JavaScript game engine. I hope this demonstrates my teachability and passion for development.



While in college, I started as a bus driver, and I worked my way through various supervisory positions until I became the Director of a bus charter rental company; managing operations, administrations, and finances. I accomplished significant goals - like increasing revenue and creating a company website - and I hired, supervised, and mentored over 30 direct reports.

Throughout my years of experience in human resources, I was always the content expert of the HRMS, HCM, ATS, and/or CRM systems due to my high interest. I would learn these systems' ins and outs, train others, and make recommendations to the executive team on alternative vendors, new functionalities, update patches, etc.



Playing the piano for over 20 years and working my way through business school and college helped sculpt my strong work ethics and focus. While working over 40 hours and being a full-time student, I completed my MBA program magna cum laude - with a 3.9 GPA.

As a kid, I spent hours building websites on Yahoo GeoCities and crafting custom maps on StarCraft's Map Editor. These maps would act as stand-alone games, with thousands of mini triggers and events, using StarCraft's assets. Professionally, I am a newcomer in the world of web and game development. But I have always been a consumer of tech and games; I am excited to bring my unique perspective to the industry.
